What is Trackula

We want you to know the digital world we live in: the reach of your data, and the consequences of being tracked over the web for your personal freedom. We defend your right to privacy and work to raise awareness about the consequences of overspreading personal information. We think this is very important to you, no matter who you are.

The Trackula on the Web browser addon shows you in an interactive visualization how data about you spreads on the web and which web-tracking companies are aware of the content you visit every day. We do that without saving, selling or even sending your personal information to anyone: all the data you generate stays on your own computer. Our only intention is that you are aware of this seemingly hidden information so that you can empower yourself with criticism over this situation.

This is an activist project that started at Medialab-Prado in Madrid.

Some tools

You have more privacy-related tools at PrivacyTools.io

LightBeam

Developed for Firefox, it shows your complete web history with all the third-parties and trackers that you have connected tom and where they were embedded.


ShareMeNot

Prevents the embedding of third-party "social buttons" (e.g., Like on Facebook) from tracking your information before interacting with the button.


Ghostery

Detects and blocks several kinds of tracking technologies, protecting your data and making websites load faster.


uBlock Origin

You control, through lists of filters, what kinds of technologies you may want to block. Has an advanced-user mode that allows for finer-grained control.


Tinfoil for Facebook

Generates an isolated web browsing session on your phone. This way, you can avoid logging into Facebook on your usual browser to try to prevent them from matching your web interactions to your Facebook profile.


PrivacyBadger

This self-learning addon is very easy to use and automatically prevents trackers from tracking you after it has enough information to categorize them as trackers (from your own browsing activity).

Privacy statement

Trackula does not just show the user the issues about privacy on the web, but actively tries to respect them on that instance. Thus, the authors of this web do not gather any personally-identifying data of users in a direct or indirect manner.

However, privacy is a complicated matter. By the very action of visiting this website, a series of technical traces may have been generated, which are impossible to avoid. Among them: you have performed a DNS request to some server outside of our control (probably your Internet Service Provider, or some public DNS, such as Google, OpenDNS or CloudFlare). In the case the DNS server didn't have the DNS reply cached, they will have performed a DNS request to CloudFlare, our Authoritative DNS provider for this domain.

The server that is actually serving this website is either Netlify or Gitlab Pages, so either of those companies might be logging accesses to this webpage, including the user's IP address, and those data the browser provides, such as User-Agent information, acceptable languages or operating system.

Given the technical difficulty, careful consideration has been taken on minimizing exposure of information to third parties. In particular, we use our own domain name and we do not load third-party resources or resources from CDNs. This prevents most of the browser tracking mechanisms.

Keep in mind that in order for this website to reach your browser, many more entities have routed it through them. In particular, your Internet Services Provider, as well as all the Autonomous Systems on the Internet that have been involved in the IP interconnect from Netlify/Gitlab through your ISP.

Netlify, Gitlab and CloudFlare offer these services self-provisioned for free, and Trackula does not receiv any kind of information or benefit, or has any ongoing deal or contract with Netlify, CloudFlare nor Gitlab diferring from the publicly available on their websites.
